How to answer well

Use the pattern that fits most often.

  1. Think about your default state, not your ideal self.
  2. Think across school, work, home, and relationships.
  3. If two answers fit often, choose C when it truly feels like both.
  4. If you built systems to cope, answer for what happens underneath those systems.
  5. D does not automatically mean neurotypical. It can also mean masking, support, familiar settings, or lower visible strain in that context.
